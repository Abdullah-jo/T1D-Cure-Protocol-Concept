Author: Abdullah M. Doumi | Protocol Architect & Innovator

The Opportunity

This document outlines a unique opportunity to pioneer a potentially curative, one-time gene therapy for Type 1 Diabetes (T1D). The proposed approach targets the root autoimmune cause of the disease, representing a significant departure from current symptom-management paradigms and addressing a multi-billion dollar unmet medical need.

Core Methodology

The strategy is based on a comprehensive protocol for the ex vivo dual-gene editing of a patient's own hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs). The design aims to engineer a durable "immune reset" through two synergistic edits:

  • CTLA4 Enhancement (Gain-of-Function): Aims to bolster the stability and suppressive function of regulatory T-cells (Tregs) using ABE8e base editing.
  • PD-1 Knockout: Designed to prevent T-cell exhaustion and restore immune homeostasis using high-fidelity Cas9.

Key Differentiators

  • First-in-Class Approach: Targets the immune system at its source (HSCs) for a potentially systemic and permanent effect.
  • Platform Potential: The core IP is adaptable to other major autoimmune diseases (e.g., MS, Lupus).
  • Translation-Ready Framework: Meticulously designed with GMP and regulatory pathways in mind to facilitate streamlined development.
