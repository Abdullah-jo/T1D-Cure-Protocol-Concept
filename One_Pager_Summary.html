A Novel Gene Therapy Platform for Type 1 Diabetes

A Preclinical Opportunity to Pioneer a Potentially Curative, One-Time Therapy

The Unmet Need

Type 1 Diabetes (T1D) is a chronic autoimmune disease with no cure, impacting over 9 million people globally. Current treatments manage symptoms but fail to address the underlying autoimmune attack, creating a significant market for a transformative, disease-modifying therapy.

The Proposed Innovation

We have architected a comprehensive protocol for a one-time therapy using a patient's own hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs), engineered with a dual CRISPR-editing strategy:

  • CTLA4 Enhancement (Gain-of-Function): Designed to boost regulatory T-cell (Treg) function for systemic immune tolerance.
  • PD-1 Knockout: Aims to prevent T-cell exhaustion, allowing the immune system to re-establish balance.

This approach is designed to create a lasting "immune reset," with the goal of protecting pancreatic beta cells from autoimmune destruction.

Strategic Value & Differentiators

  • First-in-Class Approach: Targets the immune system's origin (HSCs) rather than downstream effects.
  • Platform Potential: The core IP is adaptable to other major autoimmune diseases (e.g., MS, Lupus) and immuno-oncology.
  • Translation-Ready Design: The protocol is meticulously structured with GMP and regulatory pathways in mind, facilitating a streamlined development timeline.
